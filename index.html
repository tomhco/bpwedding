The Wedding Of

Pete and Beth

We are getting married!

Croeso! Welcome to our wedding website! We are so excited to spend our big day with you! We will send formal invitations in due course, however we will also be adding additional information to this website to hopefully make your trip to North Wales a bit easier! A lot of people have asked us ‘why North Wales’? Since we have been together, Snowdonia has been a very special place for us; we would go as far as saying it is our second home! We have shared so many adventures together up in the mountains, one of them being the sunny day in October that Pete surprised Beth mid-walk with an engagement ring at Cwm Idwal (you may recognise it from the Save The Dates)! Beth isn’t the best at walking down hills at the best of times (she blames it on the fact she has short legs) but she definitely took a lot longer getting down that day as she half-cried, half-laughed her way back to the car post-proposal! In short, Snowdonia was really the only place that we could imagine getting married and we hope to one day settle there in our own little house in the hills.

The Ceremony

The wedding ceremony will take place at 1100 at Saint Peris in the parish of Bro Eyri. The Church sits at the foot of Snowdon in the hamlet of Nant Peris. It is approximately 2 miles from the town of Llanberis. From Llanberis, drive slowly through Nant Peris and you will see the Church tucked behind the Vaynol Arms Pub on the right hand side.

Things to Do

North Wales was named the fourth best place in the world to visit in 2017 according to Lonely Planet so there are plenty of things to do while you are up there! We definitely recommend checking some out if you have time!


  • Snowdon (Yr Wyddfa) (and the rest of the Welsh 3000s!)- Arguably the best mountain in the world (says Beth, but only on a quiet day). Lots of paths up ranging from the gentle(ish) Llanberis Path to the slightly more hairy Crib Goch route!

  • Go Below- A firm favourite of ours! Hailed as the ultimate underground adventure set in an old slate mine, you zip-line through caverns, climb up vertical shafts, boat across an underground lake, traverse over an abyss, scale a waterfall or abseil to the deepest point in the UK!!!

  • Zip World Velocity, Bethesda- You soar headfirst through an old quarry at over 100mph – need we say anymore!

  • Bounce Below- Essentially a load of giant bouncy nets in an underground cavern the size of a cathedral!

  • Sygun Copper Mines- Victorian copper mine!

  • Surf Snowdonia- Surfing in Snowdon in an artificial wave pool!

  • Ffestiniog and West Highland Railway- The Welsh Highland Railway
